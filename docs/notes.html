PhD Qualifying Exam – Study Notes

How I Studied

I’d like to share how I studied for my PhD qualifying exam, in case it helps others.

Key insight: learn derivation, not memorization.

The real test: close everything and derive from scratch. If you can’t, you haven’t learned it.

Learning ≠ solving. What matters is working correctly.

Banking

Banks are not institutions — they are a collection of contracts.

  • Adverse selection & moral hazard
  • Diamond–Dybvig: liquidity + bank runs
  • Loan contracts = incentives
  • Risk is redistributed
  • Securitization = repackaging risk

Banking = contract theory.

Corporate Finance

Corporate finance = incentive design.

  • Agency problems
  • Information asymmetry
  • MM = benchmark
  • Debt = discipline

Focus: problem → mechanism

Econometrics

Econometrics = understanding estimators.

  • β vs β̂ vs β̃
  • OLS = optimization
  • Gauss-Markov logic
  • Focus on behavior, not tests

Asset Pricing

Asset pricing = pricing of risk.

  • Mean-variance
  • CAPM = equilibrium
  • APT = generalization
  • Risk → return

Behavioral Finance

Behavioral finance = systematic irrationality.

  • Limits to arbitrage
  • Bias → behavior → price
  • Sentiment & attention
